Selected Research & Reports

Computer Vision USRF 2025

Underwater Object Detection using YOLO and ResfEANet

Designed a murky water augmentation algorithm and tuned anchor boxes with YOLOv8 architectures, applying external attention modules to combat low-visibility marine imaging...

Abstract: Current object detection frameworks struggle in underwater environments due to scattering, absorption, and turbidity. This research addresses visibility limits by integrating YOLO detection with the ResfEANet feature extraction backbone. We developed a custom Murky Water Augmentation (MWA) pipeline to simulate varying degrees of light attenuation. By incorporating external attention mechanisms, the model prioritizes structural borders over background noise, resulting in a 75% improvement in mAP on standard URPC datasets.

mAP: +75%
Anomaly Detection Infosys 2025

Multi-Modal Biometric Fraud Detection and AML Compliance

Proposed a multi-layered identity verification architecture combining Optical Character Recognition (OCR) with deep biometric verification algorithms...

Abstract: Automated KYC systems are vulnerable to synthetic identities and forged physical documents. This paper details a multi-modal authentication framework that processes facial biometrics and document security features simultaneously. By combining OCR engines with deep CNN anomaly detectors, the system flags digital manipulations and synthetic document features in real-time, reducing manual AML compliance verification overhead by 60%.
